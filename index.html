How I think about this work

Most people who work in GTM either understand the strategy or can configure the tools. The gap — understanding the data infrastructure underneath, and being able to build against it — is where I spend most of my time.

"I know what ZoomInfo does under the hood. I built systems on top of the same data infrastructure that their Sales teams depend on."

Two years at ZoomInfo meant working directly with the data that powers enterprise GTM motions — enrichment pipelines, account intelligence, lead qualification workflows. I wasn't using the product as a customer. I was building internal tooling on top of it, for the people who ran revenue at one of the GTM industry's most recognised platforms.

That changes how I read a GTM stack. When I look at a company's enrichment setup or CRM routing logic, I understand not just the tooling, but the data quality and signal logic underneath it — where the gaps tend to form, and why they're hard to fix without going a layer deeper.

On AI in GTM: I've used LLMs in production for lead qualification and enrichment — not as wrappers around existing tools, but as reasoning layers that handle classification, normalisation, and personalisation at scale. The lead triage engine I built reduced manual research time by 85%. The AI data assistant I built at ZoomInfo replaced analyst requests with natural language queries, without training anyone. Both shipped. Both got used.

I also build products independently. PingHook started as infrastructure I needed for my own GTM workflows and became a standalone microservice. Owning the full lifecycle — schema design, API build, security hardening, testing, deployment — changes how you think about building internal systems too.
